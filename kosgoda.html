Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery

Established in 1978 by Dr. Upen de Silva and Dr. R. S. B Wickremsinghe, the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery in Sri Lanka
holds a significant place in conservation efforts. With a mission to protect and preserve sea turtles,
this hatchery has played a pivotal role in safeguarding endangered turtle species. Through their dedicated work,
the founders have created a haven for turtle eggs, ensuring their safe incubation and successful hatching.
The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery serves as a valuable educational center, raising awareness about the importance of turtle conservation
and inspiring visitors to contribute to these vital initiatives. With its rich history and commitment to wildlife preservation,
the hatchery continues to make a lasting impact in Sri Lanka's conservation landscape.

Location and Cost

Located about 2 kilometers north of Kosgoda, the Turtle Hatchery is an
exclusive research facility that plays a vital role in safeguarding endangered
marine turtles. This privately owned sanctuary is strategically positioned
along the picturesque coastline offering a serene environment for both the turtles and visitors. The facility carefully selects and displays a captivating collection of turtle eggs,
allowing guests to witness firsthand the remarkable stages of growth these magnificent creatures undergo. However,
the experience doesn't end there. For those willing to pay an additional fee,
there is a unique opportunity to actively contribute to turtle conservation by releasing baby turtles into the expansive ocean. This hands-on experience provides a deep sense of connection and appreciation for the natural world. To make the most of this extraordinary
encounter, it is recommended to plan a visit between March and April, when the turtles
are at their most active. During this period, visitors can fully immerse themselves in
the wonders of the Turtle Hatchery, marveling at the intricate life cycles of these gentle
beings. To access this truly unforgettable experience, an entrance fee of Rs. 500 (USD 2.5) is required. It is a small price to pay for the chance to be a part of the ongoing efforts to
protect and preserve these extraordinary creatures for future generations.

Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery

Situated along the southwest coast of Sri Lanka, the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery plays a pivotal role in the preservation of endangered sea turtles.The hatchery provides a nurturing environment for these delicate eggs, patiently awaiting the moment when the hatchlings are ready to be released into the wild. Additionally, the hatchery actively raises awareness through educational programs, emphasizing the significance of sea turtle conservation. The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery stands as a shining example of proactive conservation efforts dedicated to the protection of these extraordinary species.
