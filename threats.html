~THREATS~

There are numerous dangers that threaten sea turtles' survival. Some of the most urgent issues include habitat loss
brought on bycoastal development, pollution from plastic waste and oil spills, climate change affecting nesting
locations, entanglement in fishing gear, and illegal trading.To safeguard these amazing creatures and their delicate
ecosystems, conservation measures are essential.

Habitat Loss:

The degradation of sea turtles' crucial breeding beaches and feeding areas is brought on by coastal development and
human activity. By reducing the number of nesting sites accessible due to the conversion of these regions into resorts,
metropolitan areas, or industrial locations, sea turtle numbers are reduced and their ability
to reproduce is hampered.
TO prevent- loss for sea turtles: enforce protected areas, regulate coastal development,
promote responsible fishing practices, reduce pollution, support beach clean-ups, educate communities,
and collaborate with conservation organizations and governments to ensure effective
conservation measures are implemented.
Methods to prevent

Pollution:

Particularly prone to pollution are oil spills and plastic waste in the ocean.
Malnutrition and intestinal obstructions can result from ingesting plastic that has been mistaken for food.
Oil spills contaminate their environments, cover the bodies and shells of turtles,
affecting their buoyancy, thermoregulation, and general well-being.
TO prevent- Reduce plastic waste, recycle, and properly dispose of fishing gear. Support organizations
working to protect sea turtles and participate in beach cleanups to prevent pollution and entanglement hazards.
Methods to prevent

Climate Change:

Sea turtles suffer as a result of climate change's rising temperatures and sea levels.
Nesting beaches are getting smaller and smaller as a result of erosion or flooding. The turtles'
food sources are disrupted by variations in ocean currents and temperatures, which causes starvation
and difficulties during reproduction.
TO prevent-Reduce greenhouse gas emissions by conserving energy, using renewable sources,
and supporting policies promoting clean energy. Protect sea turtle habitats from rising temperatures
and sea levels through conservation efforts.
Methods to prevent

Fisheries Bycatch:

When sea turtles get trapped in fishing equipment like nets and longlines,
they frequently end up as unintended bycatch victims in fisheries. The turtles involved
in these encounters may sustain wounds, drown, or pass away. By reducing bycatch and
safeguarding sea turtle populations, sustainable fishing methods can be achieved by
using turtle-friendly fishing techniques like Turtle Excluder Devices (TEDs).
