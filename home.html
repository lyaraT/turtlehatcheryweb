Introduction to Sri lanka sea turtles

All of these species are designated as endangered and are protected under Sri Lankan law. The southern coast of Sri Lanka is home to many important nesting locations for sea turtles, including Hikkaduwa, Kosgoda, Bentota and Mirissa. People can engage in conservation efforts and observe the nesting process at these sites. Apart from nesting, human activities such as fishing, pollution, and habitat loss endanger sea turtles in Sri Lanka. Many organizations and government agencies have taken initiatives to protect these endangered species, such as beach monitoring, egg relocation, and public education campaigns.

Turtle Hatcheries

Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery

Situated along the southwest coast of Sri Lanka, the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery plays a pivotal role in the preservation of endangered sea turtles.The hatchery provides a nurturing environment for these delicate eggs, patiently awaiting the moment when the hatchlings are ready to be released into the wild. Additionally, the hatchery actively raises awareness through educational programs, emphasizing the significance of sea turtle conservation. The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery stands as a shining example of proactive conservation efforts dedicated to the protection of these extraordinary species.
The turtles in this hatchery:

  1. Green turtle
  2. Hawksbill
  3. Loggerhead
  4. Olive Ridley
  5. Leatherback

Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery

Located on Sri Lanka's southwest coast, the kosgoda Turtle Hatchery serves as a crucial conservation initiative for the endangered sea turtles. The dedicated team and volunteers safeguard turtle eggs collected from vulnerable nesting sites, ensuring their safety and survival. They provide a nurturing environment until the hatchlings are ready for release. Through educational programs, the hatchery raises awareness about the importance of sea turtle conservation. The kosgoda Turtle Hatchery exemplifies active conservation efforts, protecting these extraordinary marine creatures. The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery serves as a valuable educational center, raising awareness about the importance of turtle conservation and inspiring visitors to contribute to these vital initiatives. With its rich history and commitment to wildlife preservation, the hatchery continues to make a lasting impact in Sri Lanka's conservation landscape.
