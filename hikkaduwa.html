Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery

The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery in Sri Lanka is a unique conservation effort that is essential
to preserving sea turtles, which are in risk of extinction. This hatchery, which is situated along
the country's southwest coast, acts as a haven for these majestic creatures. By gathering the eggs
from delicate nesting locations, the dedicated staff and volunteers make every effort to assure the security
and survival of the turtle eggs. Until the hatchlings are strong enough to be released into the water,
they offer a loving environment. The hatchery encourages visitors to support these efforts by educating
people about the value of sea turtle conservation through its educational programs.
The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is a brilliant example of conservation
in action because of its dedication to protecting these amazing marine animals.

Location and Cost

The Turtle Hatchery is situated along the shoreline, approximately
2 kilometers north of Hikkaduwa. This privately owned research facility serves as a sanctuary
dedicated to safeguarding endangered marine turtles. Visitors to the hatchery can
observe a curated collection of turtle eggs and witness the remarkable growth
stages of these majestic creatures. For a supplementary charge, certain areas
provide the unique chance to release baby turtles into the expansive ocean.
The optimal period to visit and witness the turtles in their
habitat is during the months of March to April. During this time, one can truly
appreciate the wonders of the Turtle Hatchery and its inhabitants.The entrance fee is Rs.500(usd 2.5)

What you can do at the hatchery

1.Gain knowledge about turtle conservation: Visiting a turtle hatchery is a great way to learn about the value of turtle conservation and the difficulties that these endangered animals confront.
To learn about the measures used to safeguard turtles and their habitats, you can take part in educational activities and speak with expert professionals.

2.Observe turtle nesting and hatching: Seeing turtles nest and hatch is one of the joys of visiting a turtle hatchery. Depending on the time of year, you might be able to see turtles coming ashore to lay their eggs
or experience the wonderful moment when young turtles leave their nests and head for the water.

3.Encourage turtle release programs: Some turtle hatcheries provide the unique opportunity for baby turtle releases.the sea with turtles.
By taking part in these initiatives, you actively support conservation efforts and improve the chances of survival for these small creatures in their native habitat.

4.Check out interactive exhibits: Turtle hatcheries frequently offer engaging exhibits that reveal details about the habitat, behavior, and life cycle of turtles. To learn more about these amazing marine creatures and their ecological importance, visit the educational exhibits, movies, and visual aids available.

To safeguard the safety of the turtles and their surroundings, keep in mind that it is crucial to adhere to any instructions or rules offered by the personnel when visiting a turtle hatchery.
